## Item 1 — Business Description

Moody's Corporation is a global integrated risk assessment firm that provides credit ratings, research, data, analytical tools, software, and risk management services to financial institutions, corporations, governments, and investors worldwide.

**Two Reportable Segments:**

1. **Moody's Investors Service (MIS)** — ~53% of FY2025 revenue (~$4.1B)
   - Credit ratings on debt obligations (corporate, government, structured finance, project finance)
   - Rated issuance fees (issuer-pays model) + subscription-based monitoring fees
   - Q1 2026: Rated Issuance surpassed $2 trillion for the first time in a single quarter

2. **Moody's Analytics (MA)** — ~47% of FY2025 revenue (~$3.6B)
   - Research, data, and analytical tools (licenses + SaaS)
   - Risk management software (RMS, CreditLens)
   - Insurance and KYC/compliance solutions
   - Decision intelligence / agentic AI workflows
   - Strategic partnerships: Microsoft (Moody's intelligence in Azure AI) + AWS Marketplace

**Key competitive advantages (Moat):**
- Duopoly with S&P Global in credit ratings — regulatory recognition as NRSRO creates enormous intangible asset/barrier
- "Issuer-pays" model means ratings are mandatory for capital market access; issuers cannot skip Moody's
- MA provides switching-cost-heavy SaaS tools deeply embedded in bank risk workflows
- 16,076 employees globally with majority-owned rating affiliates in key markets
- Pricing power: persistent ability to raise prices on MA subscriptions and rating fees

## Item 1A — Key Risk Factors (Selected)

1. **Regulatory risk** — Moody's operates as an NRSRO; EU CRA regulations, SEC oversight; new rules could restrict business practices or mandate rating methodology changes
2. **Litigation exposure** — Historical exposure to lawsuits from investors over ratings accuracy (e.g., 2008 crisis legacy); ongoing risk from any high-profile default
3. **AI/data regulatory risk** — New AI regulations (EU AI Act, US executive orders) could increase compliance costs and affect MA products
4. **Issuance cyclicality (MIS)** — Rating revenue tied to debt issuance volumes; economic downturn or rising rates reduce issuance and fees
5. **Competition** — Fitch Ratings (MIS); Bloomberg, Refinitiv, MSCI (MA) compete for analytics/data market share
