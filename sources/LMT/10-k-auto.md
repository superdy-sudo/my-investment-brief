# Lockheed Martin (LMT) - 10-K Annual Report Key Highlights
Source: SEC EDGAR Filing FY2025 (Period ending Dec 31, 2025)
URL: https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/LMT/10-k-lockheed-martin-corp-files-annual-report-6a9c60e27f37.html
Direct SEC: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000936468/000162828026004195/lmt-20251231.htm

## Revenue Trend
- Q4 FY2025 Sales: $20.3 billion (vs $18.6B in Q4 2024)
- FY2025 Full Year guidance: $77.5-$80.0 billion
- Revenue mix: 72% from U.S. Government (as of 2025)
- F-35 program: 27% of total consolidated sales; 67% of Aeronautics segment sales
- Delivered 191 F-35 aircraft in 2025

## Margins
- FY2025 Cash from Operations: $8.6 billion
- FY2025 Free Cash Flow: $6.9 billion
- Q1 2026 showed margin compression: Operating profit fell, FCF dropped to -$291 million

## Moat / Competitive Advantages
- Record backlog of $194 billion (Q4 2025) - +17% YoY, covering 2.5+ years of future sales
- Unique position in U.S. defense with F-35 (sole-source program)
- Switching costs: government weapons systems take decades to replace
- Intangible assets: classified programs, proprietary technology
- Positioned for "Golden Dome" missile defense initiative
- Long-term government contracts provide revenue visibility
- 123,000 employees with deep technical and program management expertise

## Business Segments
1. Aeronautics (F-35 dominant)
2. Missiles and Fire Control (PAC-3 missiles)
3. Rotary and Mission Systems
4. Space

## Key Risks
- $1.6 billion in program losses (2025) from fixed-price developmental contracts
- Q1 2026: FCF was -$291 million (vs +$955 million in Q1 2025) - significant deterioration
- Defense budget uncertainty / sequestration risk
- SpaceX disrupting traditional defense in missile/space sectors
- F-35 production delays and cost overruns
- Supply chain disruptions and inflationary pressures
- Revenue concentration: 72% from single customer (U.S. Government)
