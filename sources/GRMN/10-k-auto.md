## Item 1 — Business Description

Garmin Ltd. designs, develops, manufactures, markets, and distributes a diverse range of navigation, communication, and information products and applications, primarily enabled by GPS technology. The company operates across five business segments:

1. **Fitness** — GPS-enabled watches, cycling computers, fitness trackers, advanced wearables
2. **Outdoor** — Handheld/wearable devices for outdoor recreation (hiking, hunting, golf)
3. **Aviation** — Panel-mounted flight systems, portable GPS, avionics
4. **Marine** — Chartplotters, sonar/fishfinder, VHF radio, autopilots
5. **Auto OEM** — In-dash navigation and connected solutions for OEM automotive customers

**Key competitive advantages:**
- Vertical integration: design, engineering, manufacturing (Taiwan primary + US/Europe) all in-house
- Over 2,100 patents issued worldwide; 1,290+ trademark registrations
- 23,000+ employees as of December 27, 2025
- Delivered 300+ million products cumulative; 20+ million in FY2025 alone
- Broad multi-segment exposure reduces single-market dependence

## Item 1A — Key Risk Factors (Selected)

1. **Supply chain concentration** — primary manufacturing in Taiwan; geopolitical risk (China-Taiwan tensions) could disrupt production
2. **Currency risk** — significant international revenue (majority of sales outside US); CHF functional currency adds complexity
3. **Competitive pressure** — Apple Watch, Samsung, Polar compete in fitness/wearables; Google/others in automotive
4. **Technology obsolescence** — rapid innovation cycles in wearables and GPS require continuous R&D investment
5. **Regulatory** — aviation segment subject to FAA and international aviation authority certifications; any regulatory change can delay product launches
