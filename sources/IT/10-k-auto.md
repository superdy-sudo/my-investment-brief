## Item 1 — Business

Gartner is the world's leading research and advisory company, serving C-suite and technology executives across enterprises, government, and nonprofit organizations in 90+ countries. Three business segments:

1. **Research** (~80% of revenue) — subscription-based access to proprietary research, benchmarks, and peer networks. Clients include CIOs, CTOs, CFOs, and other functional leaders seeking objective insights for technology decisions. Flagship: Gartner Magic Quadrant, Hype Cycle
2. **Conferences** (~10% of revenue) — large-scale executive events (Gartner IT Symposium, Gartner Security Summit, etc.) — serves as lead generation and brand reinforcement
3. **Consulting** (~10% of revenue) — short-term advisory engagements for specific strategic decisions

Competitive advantages:
- **Intangible asset (brand + data)**: Gartner Magic Quadrant and Hype Cycle are industry-standard reference frameworks; CIOs cite Gartner research in budget justifications and vendor evaluations — embedded in enterprise decision-making processes
- **Switching costs**: Multi-year subscription contracts; research is integrated into procurement workflows and IT strategy processes — high organizational switching cost
- **Scale**: Largest dedicated IT research firm globally; scale of analyst coverage (1,000+ analysts) is difficult to replicate
- **Proprietary data**: Decades of benchmarking data and peer comparisons unavailable elsewhere

ROIC exceeds WACC by >14% — strong evidence of durable competitive advantage.

## Item 1A — Risk Factors (Top 5)

1. **US Federal Government Exposure** — Federal segment showing slower CV growth; government budget cuts (DOGE-related) could impact revenue. Contract Value ex-US Federal grew 3.5% vs overall 1% in Q1 2026, showing divergence
2. **Macro Sensitivity** — Enterprise IT budgets are cut during downturns; Gartner subscription renewal rates can decline if clients reduce advisory spend
3. **AI Disruption** — Free AI research summaries (ChatGPT, Perplexity) could reduce perceived value of paid Gartner subscriptions among cost-conscious mid-market clients
4. **Negative Equity / Leverage Optics** — Aggressive buyback program has created near-zero (or negative) book equity; while net debt/EBITDA is only 0.56x, the D/E ratio metric of 47x can mislead screeners
5. **Concentration Risk** — Research segment represents ~80% of revenue and nearly all profit; any structural decline in this segment has outsized impact

## Item 7 — MD&A Financial Highlights

**Revenue FY2025:** $6,497M (+3.67% YoY from $6,267M FY2024, $5,907M FY2023)
- Revenue growth has been decelerating (FY2024: +6.1%, FY2025: +3.7%)
- Research segment Contract Value: $5.3B (Q1 2026), +1% YoY (soft; ex-Federal +3.5%)

**Operating Income FY2025:** $1,026M (margin ~15.8%, down from $1,156M/18.5% in FY2024)
- Margin compression primarily from higher compensation and technology investment costs

**Net Income FY2025:** $729M (vs $1,254M FY2024 — large one-time items affected comparison)

**Free Cash Flow FY2025:** $1,175M (vs $1,383M FY2024 — down 15%)
- FCF guidance for 2026: at or above $1.16B

**Balance Sheet Note:** Total stockholders' equity as of March 31, 2026 = $63M (near zero); driven by $9.57B treasury stock from aggressive buybacks. Net debt/EBITDA = 0.56x — manageable. Total debt outstanding = $3.0B.

**Adjusted EPS FY2025:** ~$12.00 (estimated); management guiding FY2026 adj. EPS at or above $13.25 (+12%+ CAGR target over 3 years)

**Management Commentary (Q1 2026, CEO Eugene Hall):** "Insights revenue, Adjusted EBITDA, Adjusted EPS, and free cash flow were ahead of expectations." Repurchased $535M of stock in Q1 2026. Board added $600M to buyback authorization in April 2026.
