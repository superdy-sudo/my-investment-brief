## Key Drivers of Q1 Performance

1. **Online sales growth:** +15.5% — significant acceleration, reflects omnichannel investment
2. **Pro sales:** Continued strength; Pro segment gaining share
3. **Appliances and home services:** Outperformed overall comp
4. **Spring execution:** Strong seasonal execution drove foot traffic
5. **FBM/ADG acquisition impact:** Acquisitions contributed meaningfully to total sales growth (bulk of the +10.3% total sales increase; organic comp was only +0.6%)

## Management Comments (Q1 2026 Earnings Call — May 20, 2026)

### CEO Marvin R. Ellison:

**On tariffs:**
> "We started the year with a muted view of flat growth in the home improvement sector and set guidance to take share and outperform the market. We successfully managed through the tariff environment in 2025 and we are confident in our ability to manage through the back half of 2026."
> Tariff impacts have been factored into 2026 guidance.

**On the macro environment:**
> Ellison described a **"K-shaped economy"** dynamic — higher-income consumers are spending more, while lower-income consumers are pulling back. This bifurcation is a key factor shaping Lowe's merchandising and marketing strategy.

**On the housing market:**
> Acknowledged a **"challenging housing macro"** — high mortgage rates continue to suppress housing turnover, which is a structural headwind for both Lowe's and Home Depot.

**On guidance reaffirmation:**
> The company reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance despite the macro uncertainty, signaling management confidence in Pro segment growth and online momentum.
