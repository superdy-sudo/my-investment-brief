## Item 1 — Business

Atlassian is a software company that makes team collaboration and productivity tools used by software developers, IT, and business teams worldwide. Core products:
- **Jira** — project tracking and software development workflow (supports up to 100,000 users per cloud site)
- **Confluence** — team knowledge management and wiki (supports up to 150,000 users)
- **Jira Service Management** — IT service desk (ITSM)
- **Loom** — async video communication
- **Rovo** — AI-powered search and agent layer across Atlassian products

Deployment: Cloud (primary, ~majority of ARR), Data Center (self-hosted enterprise), and legacy Server (end-of-life Feb 2024)

Business model: Low-touch, product-led growth — no traditional salesforce for SMB; land-and-expand via viral adoption within organizations. Monetization through subscription fees + Atlassian Marketplace (5,000+ apps, $3B+ cumulative sales).

Competitive advantages (from 10-K and third-party sources):
- **Switching costs**: Deeply embedded workflows; migrating from Jira/Confluence requires re-training, data migration, and workflow reconstruction — extremely disruptive for engineering teams
- **Network effects (marketplace)**: 5,000+ marketplace apps; more users attract more developers, which attracts more users
- **Brand moat**: Default choice for software teams globally — >300,000 customers in 200+ countries
- **Scale advantage**: 81%+ gross margin with minimal salesforce overhead; ~12,000 employees serving 300,000+ customers

## Item 1A — Risk Factors (Top 5)

1. **AI Disruption Risk** — AI coding tools (GitHub Copilot, Cursor) could reduce dependency on traditional project management if autonomous coding agents replace team workflows
2. **Cloud Migration Execution** — Server end-of-life completed; Data Center to Cloud migration ongoing; failure to convert DC customers to Cloud reduces ARPU growth
3. **Competition** — ServiceNow (ITSM), Microsoft (Teams + Azure DevOps), Linear, and Monday.com compete in adjacent workflow spaces
4. **Revenue Concentration** — Significant dependence on subscription renewals; any churn acceleration in a macro downturn would impact ARR growth
5. **FX Risk** — Substantial international revenue exposure; USD strength compresses reported results

## Item 7 — MD&A Financial Highlights

**Revenue FY2025:** $5,215M (+19.66% YoY from $4,359M in FY2024, $3,535M in FY2023)
- Cloud revenue: largest and fastest-growing segment
- Data Center: second-largest, migrating enterprise customers

**Operating Income FY2025:** -$130M (GAAP, due to large SBC and R&D spend)
**Non-GAAP Operating Margin FY2025:** ~25%+ (management guided)

**Free Cash Flow FY2025:** $1,416M (vs $1,415M FY2024, vs $842M FY2023) — growing and consistent
- FCF margin: ~27%

**Gross Margin:** 83.96%

**R&D Spend:** $3.21B (TTM) — heavily investing in AI (Rovo), platform unification

**Share Repurchase:** $2.5B program announced, no expiration — signals management confidence in undervaluation

**Management Commentary:** CEO Mike Cannon-Brookes: "customers sign bigger, longer-term commitments, and connect their teams and workflows on our AI-powered platform." AI credit usage growing >20% month-over-month; Rovo customers grow ARR at ~2x the rate of non-Rovo customers.
